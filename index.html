Research Interests

The physics of
the violent universe

My work centres on compact objects and their most extreme interactions — binary mergers, tidal disruption, and accretion dynamics in strong-field gravity. I work at the intersection of numerical relativity and computational astrophysics, building simulation pipelines and analytic frameworks to extract physical observables from complex dynamical systems.


A central thread connects microscopic processes — nonlinear fluid instabilities, shock dynamics, eigenmode softening — to macroscopic signals accessible through gravitational wave and electromagnetic observations. I am broadly interested in how the extreme physics of compact objects can be used as laboratories for fundamental questions in gravity, dense matter, and cosmology.

Research Projects

Bachelor's Thesis

Tidal Effects in Black Hole–Neutron Star Mergers

Supervisor: Prof. Sayan Kar, IIT Kharagpur

Aug 2025 – May 2026
  • Constructed quasiequilibrium sequences for close BH–NS binaries using the Relativistic Affine Model (RAM), characterizing hydrodynamic equilibrium of ellipsoidal stars with TOV potential in Schwarzschild spacetime.
  • Performed linear stability analysis and studied stellar eigenmode evolution for APR, SLy, and DD2 equations of state, investigating the relationship between eigenmode softening, dynamical stability, and neutron-star tidal disruption.
  • Computed tidal disruption radii and post-merger remnant disk mass fractions across mass-ratio and compactness parameter space using fluid energy and four-velocity criteria at the onset of disruption.
Independent Research

Spectral GW Signatures from Spiral Shock Instabilities in Accretion Flows

Independent Project

May 2025 – May 2026
  • Simulated low-angular-momentum shocked Schwarzschild BH accretion flows using Athena++ (2D equatorial GRHD, HRSC, Kerr–Schild coordinates) on logarithmic grids up to 768×384 across sub-Keplerian angular momenta.
  • Triggered and analyzed non-axisymmetric spiral standing shock instabilities via Fourier decomposition, tracking nonlinear growth of m=1 spiral modes, harmonic generation, saturation, and mode-coupling-driven m=2 structure.
  • Built a Python pipeline using NumPy, SciPy, h5py, and FFT-based spectral differentiation to extract relativistic quadrupole spectra, compute quadrupolar GW signatures, and correlate frequencies with spiral shock rotation dynamics.
Research Project

Nonlinear Evolution of Primordial Non-Gaussianities in the Matter Bispectrum

Supervisor: Prof. Somnath Bharadwaj, IIT Kharagpur

Mar – May 2025
  • Developed a custom parallelized Particle-Mesh N-body solver using pyFFTW and Numba to simulate nonlinear gravitational clustering, structure formation, and Fourier-space mode coupling in cosmological matter distributions.
  • Generated Gaussian and local-type primordial non-Gaussian (fNL-parameterized) initial conditions using CLASS transfer functions and 2LPT displacement fields to study inflationary signatures in large-scale structure.
  • Computed the reduced matter bispectrum across equilateral, squeezed, and folded configurations using shell-filtered Fourier estimators, quantifying survival of primordial non-Gaussian signatures against nonlinear collapse.
Research Project

Implementation of CIPSI in PySCF for Electron Correlation

Supervisor: Prof. Sabyasachi Mishra, IIT Kharagpur

Mar – Apr 2026
  • Motivated by parallels between many-body electron correlation and dense matter in neutron star interiors, implemented the CIPSI algorithm in PySCF via iterative subspace expansion with second-order Epstein–Nesbet perturbation weights, enabling adaptive determinant selection and PT2 energy corrections.
  • Characterized the onset of strong static correlation near the dissociation limit by tracking the leading determinant weight and natural orbital occupation; benchmarked PT2-corrected energies against FCI across bond dissociation curves.
